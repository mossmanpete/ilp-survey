INSTRUCTIONS

PLEASE READ THE INSTRUCTIONS CAREFULLY BEFORE STARTING THE EXPERIMENT!

This study will consist of 50 trials in which you will judge the similarity of famous people. On each trial, you will be presented with the names of three such people. One person, presented at the top of the screen, will be the reference person. Two other people will be presented below: these people will be the choices. You should use your mouse or touch screen to select which of the choices is more similar to the reference person. Here is an example in which we have replaced the people's names you will be seeing in the actual task with animals.

Reference: EAGLE

Options:


In this case, you would select "Duck" with mouse/touchscreen because an eagle is more similar to a duck than to a horse (i.e., because both are birds).

Consider each choice carefully, and respond as quickly as you are able. When you have decided, click "Submit" and you will be redirected to the next question. There are no right or wrong answers - we are merely interested in the principles which guide your decisions. If you do not know one of the people in question, feel free to look them up online, but do not feel obliged to research them in detail. If you are ready to start this task, please press the "begin" button below. Do not hit the reload or back buttons during the experiment unless you wish to exit the study.
